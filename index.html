Bikepacking Kyrgyzstan · sleep low, ride high

Ride the roof of Central Asia.

Hand-built gravel-and-tarmac bikepacking routes through Kyrgyzstan, inspired by the Silk Road Mountain Race. Loops around Issyk-Köl and wilder gravel traverses over Kegety, Kök-Ayryk and Kalmak-Ashuu to Song-Köl — each with an interactive map, day-by-day elevation profile and resupply points. Study every pass and reusable segment on the master map, browse the real Silk Road Mountain Race editions (2019–2026), or build your own line in the route planner and download the GPX.

              The race that inspired these routes

              Silk Road Mountain Race — the editions

              The Silk Road Mountain Race is a fixed-route, unsupported, single-stage race through the mountains of Kyrgyzstan — roughly 1,700–2,050 km and 25,000–36,000 m of climbing each year. Here's every edition from 2019 to 2026 with its stats and elevation profile.
