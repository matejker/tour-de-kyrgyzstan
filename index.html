Bikepacking Kyrgyzstan · sleep low, ride high

Fifteen ways to ride the roof of Central Asia.

Hand-built gravel-and-tarmac bikepacking routes through Kyrgyzstan, inspired by the Silk Road Mountain Race. Fifteen routes in three families — gentle lake & valley loops, wilder high-pass loops, and straight expert traverses over Kegety, Kök-Ayryk and Kalmak-Ashuu to Song-Köl — each with an interactive map, day-by-day elevation profile and resupply points. How these routes were planned

Sleep low, ride high

Kyrgyzstan is high — but Lake Issyk-Köl (~1600 m) and the valley towns let you camp low and warm. Days climb into the side gorges to 2500–3500 m, then drop back down to a comfortable bed for the night.

Real roads & real elevation

Every track is snapped to actual roads and trails using the open-source BRouter engine, with real SRTM elevation. Tarmac shore roads link the gravel gorge climbs. Download the GPX and ride it.

Sensible limits

Never more than 100 km in a day (usually 60–90). The lake & valley routes keep camps below ~2000 m and passes under 3500 m; the high-pass routes cross classics like Kegety (~3780 m) with a few high camps. We avoid the long Töö-Ashuu tunnel, the busy Tokmok–Balykchy (Boom Gorge) road, the Kumtor mine and the Torugart border zone.

Always near a shop

Resupply towns are flagged on every map and in every day card, so you know where the next bakery, shop or chai stop is.

Routes are planning aids generated from open data. Conditions in the mountains change fast — always carry maps, check the weather, treat your water, and ride within your limits.
